high performance

Our performance products represents disruptive technology for the green building industry. The applications we are currently developing, evaporative cooling and living systems, take advantage of the unique micro-porous network within our products. This advanced microstructure not only allows our products to retain moisture, but they can actually wick water upwards, much like a sponge. Nevertheless, our products are extremely robust, meeting ASTM standards for masonry products. In summary, our performance lines will offer structural solutions that, unlike the competition, can deliver fluids in a constant and uniform fashion, while reducing the expense and complication associated with pumps and sprayers.

Evaporative Cooling
In our cooling brick, the micro-pores can be used to retain water and this water can be used to cool structures through evaporation. This is particularly important for buildings that need to dissipate a lot of heat, such as computer data centers, hospitals, industrial facilities, or buildings located in hot climates around the world. In this application, façade or roofing materials are saturated with water and excess heat striking the surface is dissipated through natural evaporation. Water’s high heat capacity leads to effective and efficient transfer of large amounts of thermal energy by passive means alone. Our research shows that this technology will reduce cooling energy consumption by up to 30% for a typical commercial building. Plus our material’s unsurpassed capillary action will virtually eliminate the reliance on pumps, leading to lower infrastructure, maintenance, water and energy costs. This translates to lower cooling costs and earns designers LEED points for energy efficiency.
Living Systems
The emerging field of living systems, which includes green roofs and green walls, is an arena in which our material’s water management capability has great potential. In these applications, our product simultaneously performs as a growth medium, a capillary mat, and a moisture retention substrate. EnVitrum has successfully cultivated over two dozen species of plants using our material alone. The status quo for green roofs is a heavy and water intensive system consisting of up to 8 inches of soil, several layers of drainage substrate, and a complicated irrigation arrangement. Our material combines the functions of these many layers into one, simple, lightweight product that provides constant and uniform hydration throughout the system. By minimizing gravitational leeching of water, our material can also greatly reduce the quantity of water necessary for maintaining a green roof.
