advisory board EnVitrum draws knowledge from an experienced and well-rounded advisory board consisting of individuals with expertise in all relevant disciplines. Corporate Advisors
Carsten Stinn LEED Accredited Professional — Perkins+Will
Carsten is a sustainable project design architect at the global architecture firm Perkins+Will. Carsten is a licensed architect in the State of Washington and is also a LEED Accredited Professional in Building Design and Construction (“AP BD+C”). Carsten has designed numerous sustainable projects that include green wall and green roof features. The firm, Perkins+Will, is dedicated to innovation and sustainability, with each of their international offices meeting LEED Platinum standards.
Thomas Hall Serial Entrepreneur
Thom has 20 years of experience in management, accounting, sales and marketing, and business development. He founded and bootstrapped the start-up, Capitol Reservations, which was acquired by HDN in 2005. Following the acquisition, Thom served first as president and later as a consultant for HDN. Thom also has an extensive background in public policy, legislative issues, press and public relations. This includes 8 years of service to U.S. Senator Howard M. Metzenbaum, where he held the positions of both Press Secretary and Senior Legislative Assistant.
Haeryung Shin JD — Davis Wright Tremaine
Haeryung is a business and energy transactional attorney at Davis Wright Tremaine LLP (DWT), where she is focused on representing clean tech and renewable energy venture capital and private investment funds, renewable energy development companies, clean technology companies and other emerging technology and growth companies. She is the chair of DWT’s clean technology group. Haeryung has significant experience representing renewable energy investors in connection with portfolio companies including wind developers, downstream information product providers, and companies developing a wide range of clean energy technologies.
Technical Advisors
Dr. Mamidala Ramulu Professor of Mechanical Engineering, University of Washington
Dr. Ramulu is a member of the Mechanical Engineering Department’s Senior Faculty. He is a Presidential Young Investigator and a Fellow of the American Society of Mechanical Engineers. Dr. Ramulu’s research interests include mechanics of materials, fracture mechanics, fatigue, and advanced manufacturing processes. His main research activities focus on the fields of solid and fracture mechanics, and their applications in manufacturing engineering.
Dr. Joyce Cooper Professor of Mechanical Engineering, University of Washington
Dr. Cooper's background combines environmental assessment with product design and management. Her research interests include the development and enhancement of Design for Environment (DFE) methodologies and models, as related to (1) innovative, sustainable, and concurrent technology development, design, and dissemination; (2) life-cycle assessment (LCA) and environmental performance measurement; (3) Industrial Ecology. Dr. Cooper is the director of the UW Mechanical Engineering, Design for Environment Laboratory.
Dr. Per Reinhall Mechanical Engineering Department Chair, University of Washington
Dr. Reinhall’s most recent research focuses on decreasing the environmental impact of marine construction equipment. His previous research has included work on dynamical systems and mechanics with focus on the development of instrumentation, sensors, and actuators. Dr. Reinhall has a particular interest in the convergence of technology and commercialization. He has been a founding member in a number of successful technology start-ups. As Department Chair, Dr. Reinhall has been instrumental in promoting entrepreneurship within the engineering community.
